Great White

Facts:

Here are three facts about great white sharks: 1. Great white sharks are the largest predatory fish on Earth. 2. They can grow up to 20 feet long and weigh over 5,000 pounds. 3. Great white sharks have a lifespan of around 70 years.

Are They Hostile Toward Humans? Yes they are indeed hostile towards humans, they mistake humans for seals, or other prey that they usally eat.

Saw Shark

Facts:

1. Saw sharks are a type of shark that have a long, flat snout with teeth on either side. The snout is used to detect prey and to stun it before eating. 2. Saw sharks are found in the Pacific and Indian Oceans and can grow up to 5 feet long.

Are They Hostile Towards Humans? Saw sharks are not considered dangerous to humans. They are generally harmless and do not pose a threat to humans.

Whale Shark

Facts:

1. Whale sharks are the world's largest fish, measuring up to 45 feet in length and adults weigh about 41,000 pounds. 2. Their mouth alone stretches four feet wide. 3. A whale shark's mouth has over 3,000 super-small teeth.

Are They Hostile Towards Humans? Whale sharks are not considered dangerous to humans. They are often interested in human activities such as swimming, diving, and snorkeling. Many individual whale sharks have been approached, examined, and even ridden by divers without showing any sign of aggression.

Black Tiped Reef Shark

Facts:

1. The blacktip reef shark is a species of requiem shark that can be identified by the prominent black tips on its fins, especially on the first dorsal fin and its caudal fin. 2. The blacktip reef shark prefers shallow, inshore waters and is usually found over reef ledges and sandy flats. 3. It can grow up to 6 feet in length.

Are They Hostile Towards Humans? Blacktip reef sharks are generally timid and not considered highly dangerous to humans. While they can be aggressive towards other fish and other sharks, they rarely attack humans and are generally considered to be a fairly harmless species.

Bull Shark

Facts:

1. The bull shark is a large active predator. The biggest females can reach 4 meters (about 13 feet) in length and weigh as much as 316.5 kg (about 700 pounds). Males are slightly smaller, growing to a length of 3.6 meters (11.8 feet). 2. Bull sharks can live in both saltwater and freshwater environments. 3. Bull sharks have large, heavy bodies that appear very round. Their snouts are short and blunt. They have large dorsal fins on their backs, but they do not have an inter-dorsal ridge running down their backs as other sharks do. They are grey on top with white undersides. Young bull sharks often have dark tips on their fins.

Are They Hostile Towards Humans? Bull sharks are known to be aggressive towards humans and are considered one of the most dangerous sharks in the world. They are responsible for many attacks on humans in shallow waters. However, bull sharks are not specifically aggressive toward humans. If they feel threatened or provoked, they won't hesitate to strike.
