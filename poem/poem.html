Sharks

In the ocean's depths, where shadows dance, A creature prowls, in a fearsome stance.

With sleek, silver skin and piercing eyes, The shark roams free, where the current lies.

Beneath the waves, a predator's grace, A symphony of strength, swift in its chase.

Teeth like daggers, rows upon rows, The hunter strikes, where the current flows.

In ancient tales, they're a symbol of might, A timeless presence, ruling the night.

From hammerheads to the mighty great white, Sharks command respect with their primal sight.

But beyond the myths, let's see what lies, A marvel of nature, in disguise.

A delicate balance, they've come to learn, From reef to reef, the ecosystems they churn.

For in their role, as the ocean's guard, Sharks play a part, both feared and marred.

Let's strive to protect, their wondrous domain, And ensure their presence forever will remain.

So let us admire, from afar or near, The magnificence of sharks, without fear.

In their realm, let harmony thrive, As the guardians of oceans, they strive.
